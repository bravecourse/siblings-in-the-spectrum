Coping Strategies

Relax and Breathe

Life can get pretty hectic, take time for yourself

Struggling with emotions and family duty

Having a brother or sister in the Autism Spectrum can add stress to our lives. As siblings we want to help around the house, help with our siblings, and help our parents in many other ways. There are times when we feel overwhelmed with emotions, feelings of obligation, also wanting to be part of the family and an individual at the same time.

Sometimes you need to take time for yourself and refill your battery. Try some of these ideas.

  • Listen to music
  • Practice meditation
  • Practice mindfulness
  • Keep a journal or diary
  • Work on a craft project or hobby
  • Spend time with friends and other loved ones
  • Move around - exercise: dance, play a sport (team or individual)
  • Get out in nature - take a walk or hike, go to a park
  • Have a dog? Take them for a walk. Or if you have other pets, spend time with them

Talk to someone

It is important to talk with someone - whether it is one person you trust or a few people - about how you are feeling and what is on your mind. You should try to talk to your parents first, but if you don't feel comfortable doing that, you should find another person you can trust.
Here are some suggested people outside your family, are any of them someone you could talk to?

  • Teacher
  • Principle
  • Guidance Counselor
  • Your siblings therapist
